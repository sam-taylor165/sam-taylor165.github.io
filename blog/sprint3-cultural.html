What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the ability to identify your own emotions, as well as others.

Emotional intelligence can be broken down into three skillsets.

-Emotional awareness, being able to break down or name and identify one’s own emotions.
-Applying these emotions within tasks such as thinking of problem solving.
-The ability to manage emotions, regulating your own emotions whilst having the ability to help others do the same.

How is it Different From IQ?

IQ or “Intelligence Quotient” is a measure of a person’s relative intelligence. This number can be used to express the relative intelligence of a person.
EQ or “Emotional Intelligence” measures one’s ability to manage their own emotions as well as others.

Why is it Important?

EQ is important throughout everyday life but also within the workplace. Developing EQ has shown to heighten levels of collaboration and productivity. Possessing the awareness and ability to be mindful of others emotions allows people to take others strengths and weaknesses into account whilst working together.
